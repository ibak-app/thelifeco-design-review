Frequently Asked Questions

NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is a coenzyme found in every cell of the body that plays a critical role in energy metabolism and DNA repair. NAD+ levels decline significantly with age — by up to 50% between the ages of 40 and 60. Intravenous NAD+ supplementation bypasses digestive absorption limitations, rapidly replenishing cellular reserves. Clinical evidence links elevated NAD+ to improved mitochondrial function, reduced neurological aging, enhanced DNA repair, and increased energy. Sessions are administered by our medical team under close monitoring.
Telomeres are the protective end-caps on your chromosomes, often described as the biological clock of aging. Each time a cell divides, telomeres shorten slightly. Measuring telomere length provides a direct indicator of your biological versus chronological age, and identifies how your lifestyle, stress levels, and nutrition are accelerating or decelerating the aging process. Retesting at three months after your program allows us to quantify the impact of the interventions and guide ongoing lifestyle recommendations.
